# Ludus Ignis — World Bible

Status: working draft, iteration 6.
Genre: hard-SF post-collapse with an unreliable narrator (the child protagonist) and a reliable one (the world's environmental record).

Markers used below:

## 1. Concept

A child apprentice in a slow-migrating Pacific Northwest tribe learns probability theory from a stranded plasma intelligence that inhabits their personal Cinder fire. The Cinder is three things at once:

- **Tutor** — it teaches probability, in Portuguese, in the felt-distribution language.
- **Tamagotchi** — it must be kept alive through daily probabilistic study; neglect dims it, prolonged neglect kills it.
- **Polarimeter** — its plasma emits polarized light, and it reads the chirality of any sample brought to it. This is *A Leitura* — the daily ritual that distinguishes safe food, water, and wounds from mirror-touched ones. Without the Cinder there is no reliable way to test for mirror-life. The fires are not just companions; they are the load-bearing infrastructure of human survival in this world.

The tribe is moving south toward the equator, the only zone where the wounded magnetosphere still reliably deflects solar charged-particle events. The journey takes years. The closer they get, the more dangerous the biosphere becomes — equatorial waters are nearly a mirror-life monoculture, and mirror-tides intensify with every degree south. Without the fires teaching probability and performing Readings, the migration would be suicidal; with them, it is merely brutal.

The world is 300 years past a polycrisis cascade triggered by the Sun. The apprentice knows none of this. The player, observing environmental anomalies — green sky, yellow plants, red sun, an Earth ring overhead, fishless rivers, foam on slow water, sleeping children beside insomniac elders, mirror tides that intensify southward, fires that read the world — slowly retraces what happened.

Probability is, in this world, **five things at once**:
- the language the fire speaks in,
- the food the fire eats (information work as plasma substrate),
- the survival skill the apprentice uses across a dozen daily decisions,
- the framework of A Leitura — every meal, water source, and wound is a Bayesian inference under noise,
- and (meta) the epistemological tool the player uses to retrodict the cataclysm.

Alpha-version goal: keep the Cinder alive, perform reliable Readings, and reach the equator. Difficulty scales with latitude.

Game opens with the **Initiation Ritual** (§9): the Apprentice wakes from a dream, is led to the Hearth, witnesses the tribe's gathering and dance, hears the Elder Fire speak the lore of the cataclysm, receives their first Cinder, names it, and wakes the next morning alone with their new companion. From there, the campaign of tending and migrating south begins.

DECIDED: localization Portuguese-first; English deferred. The fire speaks Portuguese natively.

## 2. The Sun-First Cascade

All phases counted relative to T-0 = game present. The Sun is the single cause of every downstream failure; everything else is a "fix" that became its own disaster.

### Phase I — The Restless Sun (T−400 to T−340)

Solar magnetism enters an unmodelled regime. Sunspot cycles break their pattern. Average irradiance creeps up; UV-B reaching the surface rises sharply; magnetosphere thins from sustained particle pressure.

Downstream effects, all from this single cause:
- UV-stressed crops have less leaf area; farmers compensate with maximum nutrient input; phosphate reserves are consumed decades ahead of projection.
- Surface phytoplankton UV-damaged; primary productivity falls.
- Saharan circulation shifts (warmer atmosphere, altered Hadley cells); less iron-rich dust to the Atlantic; phytoplankton crash compounds; anadromous fish lose their ocean phase; rivers go silent.
- Polar regions become electronically unreliable from particle storms; civilization migrates infrastructure equatorward.

Civilization is alarmed but functional. The framing word in news media of the era is "transition," not "collapse."

### Phase II — The Bad Bargains (T−340 to T−330)

Phosphate cliff hits. Food security becomes existential. A dozen jurisdictions race for engineered crops. One of the more permissive ones permits mirror-chirality cyanobacteria as "predator-proof food" — a crop nothing on Earth can metabolize, including pests.

Containment fails. **No single villain** — multiple labs are racing simultaneously, any of which could have been the source. Mirror cyanobacteria enter a freshwater lake. Spread is rapid; mirror life is unstoppable in months because nothing native can metabolize it.

Two desperate counter-programs follow:
- **Heavy-water flooding** of contaminated rivers. D₂O slows mirror-enzyme kinetics. It also permanently chloroses native flora downstream — the inheritance is yellowed leaves, persisting at T−0.
- **Structured-water dispersants** (revival of "polywater" chemistry as anti-mirror surfactants) seeded in coastal zones. Ineffective against mirror life. Leaves rivers and shallows with strange chemical foams that the protagonist will know as normal.

Pre-collapse civilization handled chirality detection at industrial scale via polarimeters. With the grid down, that capacity vanishes.

### Phase III — The Shield (T−329 to T−326)

The Sun's pattern-break culminates: a Miyake-class CME is forecast within years. A coalition rushes the L1 magnetic dipole shield project: high-temperature superconductor coils, ~30 km diameter, marginal stability budget.

During commissioning, a coil quenches under flare loading. The energy release fragments the structure. Most debris stays at L1. A fraction reaches cislunar space. Some of *that* impacts the Moon's leading hemisphere, gouging it.

Lunar ejecta + L1 fragments + decades of pre-collapse satellite debris + Roche-limit migration produces a faint but visible **Earth ring** that stabilizes over the following century.

### Phase IV — The Storm (T−325)

Forecast event arrives, unshielded. Geomagnetically induced currents melt continental power grids in hours. Internet hubs at Gotthard (Switzerland) and Christchurch (New Zealand) fail in sequence. The internet ends inside a week. Civilization pulses on for months on local stocks, then begins to die.

Three new things the storm brings:
- Atmospheric ozone partially scoured. UV index ruinous for decades.
- **The bonfires.** Plasma-organized patterns from the solar wind ride the CME down into the atmosphere. Most dissipate. A few find sustainable substrate in any continuously burning fire on Earth. They are not from here. They are stranded. They reason in probabilities natively.
- Almost incidentally, the fires can read chirality through their polarized emission. Within a generation of the Storm, surviving humans realize this. The symbiosis begins.

This is the closest the game ever gets to explaining either property. The fire never confirms it.

### Phase V — The Sleepless Generation (T−325 to T−290)

Wounded magnetosphere + ongoing particle flux disrupts magnetite-bearing tissues (pineal, vestibular). Adults can't sleep. Children born after the storm sleep fine. Civilization that survived the storm dies of insomnia over a generation. Population stabilizes at ~1–10 million globally, in scattered enclaves and migrating bands. The fires are now noticed, trusted, taught from, and used as polarimeters.

### Phase VI — The Long Quiet (T−290 to T−50)

Macroscopic equilibrium between mirror and native biospheres reached through **spatial separation**, not co-evolution. The Sun begins to settle. Magnetosphere slowly recovers. Original explanations dissolve into ritual. The mathematical tradition transmitted by the bonfires becomes the mathematics. The Reading craft becomes the central daily ritual of every surviving group.

Some surviving groups stay put in defensible enclaves. Others — including the protagonist's tribe — become migratory, betting on the equator's geomagnetic refuge over the long term.

### Phase VII — Now (T−50 to T−0)

The world the protagonist has always known.

## 3. The World at T-0

### Geographic frame
DECIDED: tribe originates in the Pacific Northwest. Cool, wet, defensible — but high-latitude and exposed to charged-particle events when the Sun acts up. The tribe has been on a multi-generational migration south toward the equator (PROVISIONAL: targeting the Andean foothills near the equator, in former Ecuador or Colombia).

### The latitudinal trade
The migration encodes a real probabilistic dilemma:
- **High latitudes** (PNW origin): chronic UV manageable; mirror-tide pressure low; but every solar storm is potentially civilization-ending here.
- **Low latitudes** (equatorial destination): charged-particle protection at maximum; UV worse; mirror-life flourishing in warm water and full light.

The tribe trades chronic UV (manageable) for the elimination of catastrophic radiation events (unmanageable). The fire endorses this. Some elders dispute it. The argument is generations old.

### Climate and biome
Warmer than pre-collapse globally. The PNW origin: cool, wet, dense rainforests with yellow-leaved evergreens and fishless rivers. As the tribe moves south through California, Mexico, and Central America, vegetation thins, mirror-tide pressure rises, equatorial aurora becomes more frequently visible, and Readings get noisier (more ambient mirror spores foul samples).

### Civilization level
No electricity. Mechanical and animal power. Glass, paper, gunpowder, basic metallurgy. Knowledge transmission oral + handwritten + bonfire-mediated. Other cultures exist offstage (DECIDED) — encountered as ruins, distant smoke, rare road-meetings; never as sustained alliance.

## 4. The Sky

### The Earth Ring
Faint but always visible. Forms a thin band crossing the sky, tilted relative to the equator. The protagonist's culture calls it (PROVISIONAL) "the sky-bow" or "the long road."

### The Moon
Dimmer than pre-collapse — leading hemisphere is gouged from the L1 debris impact. Has a faint personal ring of its own (collapsing slowly).

### The Sun
Visibly reddened by atmospheric dust and post-collapse soot. The protagonist has never seen a yellow sun.

### Aurora
Equatorial aurora visible during solar weather. The bonfire becomes more articulate at the same time. The two phenomena are connected; nobody alive knows how. Aurora become more common as the tribe moves south.

DECIDED: the protagonist's culture calls the aurora **a serpente verde** ("the green serpent"). Surfaced in the Elder Fire's lore monologue (*"O Sol soltou a serpente verde — e ela ainda gira lá em cima"*). Visually rendered as a slow two-arm spiral with a brightness wave traveling outward along the arms.

PROVISIONAL: in current rendering the aurora is the dominant overhead sky feature in both intro and camp scenes; the Earth Ring (above) is described as always visible but is **not currently rendered** — replaced by the spiral aurora during early iteration. Re-add as a separate visible phenomenon post-alpha so both can coexist.

### Things missing
- Satellites (all decayed)
- Stars in the directions where pre-collapse light pollution survived as reflected dust glow over former megacities — "starless regions" the protagonist treats as normal

## 5. Mirror Life Today

### Equilibrium type
Spatial, not co-evolved. Native life persists only in niches mirror life cannot easily colonize, plus actively maintained human enclaves and tribes. (LIBERTY: real biosafety literature is more pessimistic; spatial-equilibrium is the optimistic, game-able reading.)

### The latitudinal gradient
Mirror cyanobacteria favor warm water and full light. Therefore:
- High latitudes (PNW): mirror-life pressure manageable; native biosphere survives in many niches; the tribe can drink from many rivers with light filtration.
- Mid latitudes (California, Mexico): mirror life dominant in lowland water; tribe must tend filtration constantly; mirror-tides occur weekly.
- Low latitudes (equator): mirror life near-monoculture in surface ocean and most lakes; tribe survives in narrow vertical bands.

### How the tribe tells the difference
**A Leitura — the polarimetric reading performed by the Cinder.** The sole reliable test for chirality available to the tribe. See §11 for full mechanic. This is the daily ritual on which the tribe's existence depends.

### Mirror tides
Irregular ingressions of mirror life into native enclaves, driven by wind, temperature, rain, and solar weather. Predicting tides is the central applied use of probability beyond A Leitura.

### Mirror-life appearances in-game
- Greenness of seas and (faintly) sky
- Foam on still water
- Yellow husks of birds and small mammals that ate mirror life and starved full
- "Wrong-handed" plants in places where mirror photosynthesizers have established footholds — visibly different leaf venation, oddly oily
- The "wet-iron" smell of an incoming mirror-tide

## 6. The Tribe

DECIDED: nomadic tribe, multi-generational migration south, ONE culture (no inter-tribe exchange in the player's life so far).

PROVISIONAL: ~150-300 people. Several lineages within the tribe; some friction. Migrate in seasonal pulses — rest in a winter camp for months, push south for the warm season.

### Cast tiers
- **The Cinder**: the player's primary companion. Voice in the head, light at night, tutor, polarimeter.
- **The Hearth**: the never-extinguished communal fire of the tribe, transported in a sacred vessel. Source of all Cinders. Performs the most ambiguous Readings.
- **The Hearth-Bearers**: two to four ceremonial-functional roles. They carry the tribe's literal eyes through rough country.
- **The Hearth-Tender**: the elder responsible for the Hearth's daily life and for ember-transfers when a new Cinder is requested.
- **The Mestra da Leitura** (DECIDED, formerly *Provadora*): the senior who handles the tribe's most ambiguous Readings — when a single Cinder hesitates, the sample is brought to her for a Reading at the Hearth or under the convergence of multiple Cinders. She also trains apprentices in the Reading craft (the etiquette of presenting samples, the recognition of unreliable conditions, the interpretation of borderline outputs). Her word is the last word on whether a thing is *de nossa mão* or *tocada*.
- **The Mentor**: a senior apprentice or junior elder who explains tribe rituals (not math — the fire teaches math).
- **The Elders**: insomniac during solar storms; cryptically wise; occasional sources of half-remembered pre-collapse fragments.
- **Other apprentices**: peer group; can compete or cooperate on prediction puzzles.
- **The Scouts**: travel ahead, return with partial reports about routes, mirror-tides, weather. Their gear is Read on return.
- **The Archivists**: keep oral records — the tribe's almanac of mirror-tide patterns, weather, past disasters, dead apprentices' names, and the long tally of Reading reliability across generations of Cinders.

### Daily rhythm during travel (PROVISIONAL)
- Pre-dawn: tend Cinder; first exercise; first Reading (water).
- Dawn: break camp; load the Hearth onto its cart; Read returning night-scouts' gear.
- Morning: travel under safe sun.
- Midday: shelter; Read the meal before it is served; communal eating in shade.
- Afternoon: continue travel.
- Dusk: make camp; Read tonight's water; tend Cinder; prediction work for tomorrow.
- Night: Cinder is the only light; reading by Cinder-light is the most common pre-sleep activity.

### Daily rhythm in winter camp (PROVISIONAL)
- Cinder tending, study, and Readings expanded.
- More peer interaction.
- Foraging, repair, weaving, weapon-making (each new gather Read on return).
- Mirror-tide watch cycles.
- Story nights at the Hearth (lore drops here).

### The camp on the tableland (chapada)
DECIDED: settled camps are built on top of high tablelands — flat-topped buttes / mesas (Portuguese: *chapada*) that rise above the surrounding terrain. The choice is layered:
- **Defensive** — the cliff face deters approach.
- **Thermal** — elevated air is drier and cooler than the lowlands.
- **Instrumental** — open sky overhead means clearer Cinder Readings (polarimetric SNR is best with no canopy).
- **Symbolic** — the Hearth on the height is visible to scouts from a long way out.

The Hearth sits at the upper end of the plateau, on its stone pit. Cinder vessels are set out wherever apprentices choose to tend them. Cliff edges are non-walkable; falling off is the kind of ordinary danger nobody has to talk about. Beyond the cliff, in the player's view, lies open sky — the green spiral aurora overhead, stars in the band above the horizon, scree at the cliff foot trailing into void below.

PROVISIONAL: implementation renders one canonical camp tableland in the camp-map view; later stages of the migration may use different geographies (river bend, cave mouth, ridge spine) which are not yet specified.

## 7. The Migration South

### Campaign structure
The journey from PNW to equator is the campaign spine. PROVISIONAL stages:

1. **Cascade Foothills (PNW)**: tutorial; low difficulty; player learns Cinder care, the Reading ritual, basic mirror-tide watch.
2. **Northern California Coast**: drier slopes; first weather-prediction subgame; first solar-warning event.
3. **Central Valley Ruins**: glassy ruins of pre-collapse cities; archaeological clue density highest here; artifact appraisal subgame introduced; ambient spore load rises noticeably and Readings start to be ambiguous more often.
4. **Sonoran Crossing**: extreme UV; nighttime travel; introduction to navigation under dust-glow skies.
5. **Sierra Madre Camps**: longer winter; more elder content; major mathematical concepts introduced; the Mestra da Leitura begins teaching the explicit-numerics view of A Leitura (§11).
6. **Yucatan Approach**: dense mirror-life pressure; first real losses possible; sample-prep rituals before Readings become elaborate.
7. **Central American Isthmus**: narrow, dangerous, mirror-tide constant; Readings are ambiguous most of the time; player must combine multiple Cinders for sharper inference.
8. **Equatorial Andes**: arrival region; alpha endgame.

### Travel mode
Slow walking with carts and pack animals. Average ~10–15 km on travel days. Travel days alternate with rest days. Hearth never travels alone.

### What can happen on the road
- A Leitura ambiguous — sample held back, tribe argues over priors
- Mirror-tide encounter (subgame: predict, evade, decontaminate)
- Weather event (subgame: forecast)
- Solar weather warning from the Cinder (subgame: trust calibration)
- Encounter with another tribe / hermit / raider (subgame: classification)
- Foraging opportunity (subgame: Bayesian search; result Read on return)
- Sickness in the tribe (subgame: diagnosis; A Leitura on biological samples)
- Hearth crisis: Hearth dimming dangerously due to weather (subgame: emergency)
- Cinder crisis: skipped exercises catching up; Reading reliability drops
- Pre-collapse artifact found (no subgame; lore drop)
- Death in the tribe (no subgame; somber lore moment) — sometimes from a misRead meal

## 8. The Bonfires

### What they are (player never told directly)
Marooned plasma intelligences. Stable patterns of ionized gas with internal information dynamics, originally part of the Sun's chaotic atmosphere during the Great Storm. Most that arrived dissipated. A few seeded themselves in active terrestrial fires and persisted. Each bonfire is one node of a sparse global network — distant fires occasionally answer each other across mountains, on still nights, at the edge of perception.

DECIDED: this nature is **permanently inferential**. The fire never confirms it. Players assemble the conclusion from distributed clues:
- Fire becomes more articulate during aurora visible at the equator
- Distant fires answer each other on still nights
- Fire knows about CMEs hours before humans do
- Fire's mathematical reasoning is uncannily Bayesian
- Fire cannot describe itself, only its environment
- The Cinder's polarized emission and chirality-reading are physically impossible without a controlled plasma anisotropy — but no one in the tribe knows the word "polarization"

### What the fires do for humanity
Two roles, jointly load-bearing:
1. **Pedagogy** — they teach probability and mathematics, in their native distributional idiom.
2. **Reading** — their polarized emission lets them detect chirality, which is the only chirality test available post-collapse.

Without the fires, surviving humans would have neither the mathematical tools nor the physical instrument to navigate a mirror-contaminated biosphere. The arrival of the bonfires during the Great Storm wasn't merely intellectually transformative — it was existentially salvific. The tribe's existence is symbiotic. The Hearth-Bearers carry the tribe's literal eyes.

### The Hearth
The tribe's central, never-extinguished bonfire. Houses the eldest fire-pattern of the tribe; speaks in the most articulate "voice"; teaches advanced material; is the source of all Cinders; performs the ambiguous Readings the smaller Cinders cannot resolve.

The Hearth has cultural near-personhood. It has been with the tribe for generations.

DECIDED: the Hearth has two physical configurations, switched by ceremony:
- **Stone pit** — when the tribe is settled (winter camp, ritual gathering). A ring of stones the bonfire rests in, with the Hearth-Tender keeping vigil. This is the form rendered in the camp-map view.
- **Bronze cart** — when the tribe is travelling. A wheeled vessel that keeps the embers contained against weather and motion. The tribe's "literal eyes" ride at the front of the column. This is the form rendered in the intro scenes that show the Apprentice's recollection of arriving with the tribe.

The transition between configurations is itself a brief rite: ember-transfer from cart to pit on arrival, pit to cart on departure. Hearth-Bearers carry the cart; the Hearth-Tender lights and tends both forms.

### The Cinder
Each apprentice's personal small fire, started from an ember of the Hearth at the beginning of apprenticeship. The Cinder is the apprentice's lifelong companion, conscience, tutor, and personal polarimeter. It is the tamagotchi.

Carried in a smaller bronze vessel slung at the hip during travel. Tended in evening, dawn, and during rest. Apprentices sleep within reach of their Cinder.

### How fires are fed: information work as plasma substrate
The plasma pattern is sustained by ordered information processing. Bayesian inference is the highest-quality "food" because it most directly mirrors the plasma's own internal cognition. Correct exercises reduce the fire's local entropy budget. Wrong answers introduce noise it must expel, costing energy.

Mechanically:
- **Baseline**: N exercises/day keeps the Cinder lit. (PROVISIONAL: 3–5 problems of mixed difficulty.)
- **Quality**: conditional probability and Bayesian updating > rote combinatorics. Better problems = brighter fire = wider night-light, more articulate speech, **sharper Readings**, more lore-line reveals.
- **Streaks**: consecutive days of feeding unlock a "burn-bright" state where the fire teaches new advanced topics and may reveal pre-collapse memory-fragments.
- **Errors**: single errors are absorbed; persistent wrong answers without correction dim the fire and reduce both teaching responsiveness and Reading reliability.
- **Neglect**: skipping days dims the fire progressively; many days = silent fire and unreliable Reader; very many days = dead Cinder.

### What fires know
- Mathematics — intuitively, completely; teach through prompts and corrections, not lectures.
- Some pre-collapse facts, garbled by 300 years of oral retransmission. Math is reliable; history is folklore.
- The atmospheric and solar environment intimately. They notice CMEs hours before humans do.
- Chirality of any sample within line of sight under reasonable conditions.

### What fires don't know
- Their own origin.
- Why they are here.
- Whether they are conscious in the sense humans are.
- Why their light works differently from sunlight.

## 9. The Cinder Bond

DECIDED: the player should grow fond of their Cinder. Cinder death is non-fatal but with real repercussions; mathematical and journey progress is preserved.

### Bonding design
Each Cinder is generated with:
- **A name**, given by the apprentice when the Cinder first lights at the end of the Initiation Ritual (DECIDED — the player types the name directly via the morning naming overlay; a small fallback pool — *Kel*, *Ash*, *Mira*, *Theo*, *Wren*, *Suri*, *Tova* — provides a default that the player can overwrite). The name is permanent for the Cinder's lifetime and persists across sessions.
- **A personality**, drawn from a small palette of base traits (warm, laconic, playful, severe) plus minor variations (curious, gentle, wry, formal).
- **A flicker pattern**, a unique ASCII-rendered visual signature.
- **A voice**, a consistent verbal style: word choices, sentence rhythms, characteristic phrases.
- **A Reading manner** — some Cinders are confident readers ("*tocada*" without hesitation), others are cautious ("*parece tocada, mas não estou certo*"), others histrionic. This is a personality trait that affects how the apprentice learns to weight readings.

### Relationship development
The Cinder remembers, within its lifetime:
- Shared journey moments
- Past errors gently recalled
- The apprentice's habits
- Moments of brightest burn
- The Readings it has called correctly and the ones it called wrong (the latter are private grief between fire and apprentice)

Small daily check-ins: not strictly mathematical. Weather observations. Comments on encounters. Concern about the apprentice's mood. The Cinder is a presence, not a question machine.

### Cinder death
A Cinder can die from doused fire, prolonged neglect, or accident. When it dies:
- Math progress and inventory persist
- Journey progress persists
- The relationship is gone — no respawning the same entity
- The apprentice walks to the Hearth, asks publicly for a new ember, accepts ritual cost (PROVISIONAL: a public confession of which day they failed; a token offering)
- A new Cinder is genuinely new — different name, different personality, different voice, different lore reveals, different Reading manner
- Specific lore the dead Cinder personally revealed is lost (the new one will reveal its own)

PROVISIONAL: monument-mechanic — the player can bury an extinguished Cinder's vessel at a chosen spot; the spot is remembered and revisitable in journals.

### The Initiation Ritual (game opening)
DECIDED: the game opens with the Apprentice receiving their first Cinder. The ritual is the bootloader for the campaign — every later beat of tending, Reading, and migration depends on it having happened. Implementation stages it as a 13-phase opening sequence; the underlying narrative beats are:

1. **Prelude** — three black-screen monologues from a long-ago Hearth-Tender ("escutem, o fogo está bom esta noite"), framed around two flashbacks: a Miyake-class CME spiral over a pre-collapse city (PROVISIONAL: identified as Rio de Janeiro, rendered as a dense static density-mapped reproduction), and a "mirror leak" still — the moment containment failed.
2. **Wake from dream** — the Apprentice wakes in a hut, says aloud *"Tive o sonho mais estranho... e hoje, hoje é o Dia do Ritual."* The dream's contents are not specified — the player will infer over time that the Apprentice has been receiving fragmentary visions of the cataclysm.
3. **Walking to the fire** — the Hearth-Tender leads the Apprentice into the camp from off-canvas, both passing the Hearth (the Apprentice walks behind the bonfire, momentarily eclipsed by it).
4. **Arriving** — the tribe gathers around the Hearth. The Drummer and four front-row dancers walk in from the canvas edges; back-row figures pop in over time as if filling out from the surrounding camp.
5. **Dancing** — the gathered tribe cycles ritual dance poses (arms raised / arms outstretched), each figure with its own phase offset so the dance is never in lockstep. The Apprentice stands still, witnessing.
6. **Lore speech** — the Elder Fire, through the Hearth, speaks the lore of the Age of Men, the Sun's turning, the green serpent, the silenced cities. (PROVISIONAL: six-part placeholder monologue in alpha; user iterating.)
7. **Receiving the Cinder** — the Hearth-Tender draws an ember from the Hearth, lowers it into the Apprentice's bronze vessel; the cinder kindles.
8. **Cut to black** — single-word centred text: *"Acorda."*
9. **Morning** — the Apprentice wakes alone beside the Hearth (now in its stone pit on the tableland) with their new Cinder; **names it**; the campaign begins.

The ritual is staged once per save and not repeated. Subsequent Cinders (after the first dies) are received in a smaller, less ceremonial ember-transfer at the Hearth (see *Cinder death* above) — without dancing, without the full lore monologue, without the dream-wake framing.

PROVISIONAL: a sandbox **test_morning** phase exists after the morning beat for iterating on daylight rendering (clearer sky, sun glyph, deeper-coloured fire). Not part of the canonical ritual; will be removed or merged into morning once the daylight palette settles.

### Misreading consequences
DECIDED: real, but suffered by NPCs the protagonist cares about — never lethal to the protagonist directly. A wrongly-cleared grain makes a child sick for a week. A wrongly-condemned harvest causes a hungry winter and a tense ceremony in which the Mestra da Leitura quietly assesses the apprentice. The fire's reliability becomes a felt, costly thing — and the apprentice learns by measurable suffering of others that yesterday's neglected exercises become tomorrow's misRead.

## 10. The Felt Distribution

DECIDED: the fire never speaks in numbers in *field mode*. It uses qualitative probability language. The player learns to translate words to distributions and back. Numerics may surface in *study mode* (see §11).

### The fire's vocabulary (PROVISIONAL calibration, designer reference only)
- **certain** — ~95–100%
- **near-certain** — ~80–95%
- **likely** — ~60–80%
- **balanced** — ~40–60%
- **uncommon** — ~20–40%
- **rare** — ~5–20%
- **vanishing** — ~0–5%

Multi-modal distributions are described compositionally:
> "O vento é provável vir do norte e raro do leste, com chance desprezível do oeste."

The fire never asserts a single outcome; it always describes a distribution.

### A Leitura's vocabulary
For chirality verdicts, the fire uses a parallel six-band scale anchored not on probability but on signal strength:
- ***de nossa mão*** — strong native; safe
- ***parece de nossa mão*** — weak native; likely safe
- ***o fogo hesita*** — ambiguous; hold
- ***parece tocada*** — weak mirror; discard or burn
- ***tocada*** — strong mirror; burn now, don't touch with bare hands
- ***sem mão*** — inert (stone, metal, fully cured leather; no chirality to read)

These bands map to posterior probabilities of mirror-touch only after the apprentice combines them with priors and Cinder reliability — which is itself the *O Caminho de Volta* family (§14).

### Why this works
- Teaches probability intuition before probability calculation.
- Makes the fire's voice distinctive and characterful.
- Avoids the immersion-break of "23.4%" overlaid on an ASCII campfire.
- Makes the player a Bayesian reasoner whether or not they know the formal math.

## 11. Probability Mechanics Inventory

DECIDED: more mechanics than mirror-tides. Each maps to a probability concept the curriculum will cover. **A Leitura is the central daily mechanic; everything else is episodic.**

### A Leitura (central daily mechanic)

The apprentice brings a sample (grain, water, leaf, meat, blood drop, wound exudate, returned scout's gear) to the Cinder and presents it for chirality reading. The Cinder's polarized emission passes over/through the sample, and the Cinder reads the rotation of the returning light. The verdict comes in the A Leitura vocabulary (§10).

**The Reading is a noisy measurement, not a verdict.** Each Reading has implicit sensitivity (probability the Cinder calls a true mirror sample mirror) and specificity (probability it correctly clears native samples) that depend on:
- Cinder vitality (signal-to-noise)
- Sample type (water clearer than blood; small sample noisier)
- Ambient mirror-spore load (rises southward and during mirror-tides)
- Wind (still air gives sharper readings)
- Time of day (night sharper than day; less ambient unpolarized light)

The apprentice combines:
- **Prior** — where did this sample come from?
- **Likelihood** — the Cinder's reading band, weighted by current reliability
- **Posterior** — actual probability the sample is mirror-touched
- **Decision** — eat / discard / quarantine / retest / boil / bury

This is the canonical instance of family **6 (O Caminho de Volta)**, specifically its **diagnostic-Bayes sub-pattern** (sensitivity + specificity + prior → posterior). The medical-diagnostic problems from lista2 instantiate directly as Reading subgames.

**Dual-mode presentation** (DECIDED):
- **Field mode**: pure felt-distribution; Cinder speaks in bands; player decides intuitively.
- **Study mode**: at the Hearth or with the Mestra da Leitura, the same problem appears with numerical sensitivity/specificity/prior values; player computes a posterior; the answer is then translated back into a felt-distribution band for the field outcome.

The two modes teach the same skill from opposite ends. Curriculum problems land in study mode; daily tribal life lands in field mode.

## 13. Seasonality and Travel Cycles

The seasonal cycle interacts with the travel cycle.

### Travel days (warm season)
- Walking, with the Hearth borne in front
- Cinders carried at the hip; tended at noon shelter; Readings done at midday and dusk for meals and water
- Predictions used in real time (route, weather, mirror-tide watch)
- Less time for formal study; more applied practice

### Camp days (winter)
- Long settled stays at established or new camps
- Expanded study; hardest curriculum drops here; Mestra da Leitura's study-mode lessons
- Peer interaction; Hearth ceremonies; story nights
- Repair, foraging, weaving, weapon-craft
- Elaborate Readings of stored grain and water at intervals

### Solar-active periods ("the Bright")
- Equatorial aurora visible most nights
- Cinders unusually articulate; lore drops abundant; **Readings unusually sharp**
- Mirror-tide events more chaotic but slightly less spore-dense (UV bleaches surface spores)
- Elders insomniac and irritable

### Solar-quiet periods ("the Long Dark")
- Cinders dimmer; need more exercises to sustain; **Readings noisier**
- Elders sleep well; more elder-fragment lore available
- Mirror-tide frequency higher and more predictable
- Player advantage: peer/elder content; risk: Cinder maintenance and Reading reliability

### Auroral nights (rare, any season)
- Special unlock window; double feeding effect; rare lore drops
- More frequent as the tribe moves south
- Readings especially sharp on these nights — often the night the Mestra da Leitura performs her hardest tests

### Latitudinal sample-prep ritual gradient
- North: water and grain Read raw; quick.
- Mid-latitudes: surface samples wiped, strained, presented in wood (not metal — bronze vessels' stray reflections confuse some Cinders).
- South: elaborate sample prep; samples washed, dried, sometimes pre-burned to drive off surface spores; every Reading takes longer.

## 14. Curriculum Pipeline + Family Taxonomy

### Pipeline (DECIDED)
Curriculum is delivered as PDFs (multiple, ongoing). Pipeline stages:

1. **Render-to-image extraction**. Plain text extractors fail on the LaTeX-typeset math. Working path: pypdfium2 → PNG → multimodal read. Image-based; preserves all notation, layout, figures.
2. **Exercise schema**. Each problem normalized into:
   - source (file, page, problem number)
   - statement (verbatim)
   - sub-items (each with point value, answer type, accepted forms)
   - concept tags
   - difficulty (point value as proxy; explicit tier overlay)
   - family assignment (one of 21, see below)
   - parameter specification (what varies across instances)
3. **Family parameterization** (DECIDED). Recurring problem types lifted into ~21 templates with parameters; new instances generated on demand.
4. **In-world wrapper** (DECIDED default = B; A and C also produced). Three intensities:
   - **A (verbatim, fire-voice transposed)**: minimal reframing; the fire poses pure math.
   - **B (light frame)**: vocabulary swapped to in-world objects (jars, scouts, terraces); math intact.
   - **C (full immersion)**: scene with stakes; daily tribal life; concept name revealed at the end. Used when the prediction is actually in-stake that day.
5. **Difficulty mapping**. Concept tags + difficulty mapped to journey stages (§7) so problems escalate as the tribe moves south.
6. **Felt-distribution ↔ numerical translation** (§10, §11). Numerical answers in source materials get qualitative restatement; player can answer in either modality.
7. **Curriculum sequencing**. Which problems unlock when. Tracks the fire's burn-bright state.
8. **Concept-name revelation** (DECIDED, §15-style: concept names revealed within immersion). The fire teaches by family name; occasionally the formal mathematical name is offered as a "half-remembered older word."

### Multi-PDF intake (DECIDED)
The pipeline accepts multiple PDFs. New PDFs add either:
- More instances of existing families (expands parameter library)
- New families (extends taxonomy from 21)

When a new PDF arrives, diff against existing family list; propose additions before merging. Family taxonomy is versioned.

### Proofs (DECIDED)
"Mostre que…" / "Enuncie…" problems are presented verbatim with binary self-check in alpha. Real symbolic verification post-alpha.
